# Prague Launch Scorecard

## 1. Validation principle

The launch is not validated by likes, compliments or waitlist size. It is validated by paid attendance, repeat behaviour, Captain reliability, safety and contribution margin.

## 2. 90-day targets

### Demand

- 100 qualified founding waitlist leads
- 50 unique paying families
- 6 to 10 completed paid Drops
- 80% target occupancy
- 35% of families attending a second Drop within 90 days
- 25% of new paying families arriving through referral by the end of the period

### Supply

- 10 Captain Parent candidates
- 4 approved active Captains
- 3 repeatable Drop formats
- At least 2 Drops completed without Nico as the lead host

### Quality

- Average parent rating of 4.6 out of 5 or higher
- 80% or greater “would book another Drop” response
- No serious preventable safety incident
- 100% of incidents and near misses documented

### Economics

- 35% to 50% contribution margin target
- No-show rate below 10%
- Refund and credit cost below 8% of Drop revenue
- Founder operating hours measured for every Drop

## 3. Lead funnel

Track every lead through these stages:

1. New waitlist lead
2. Qualified by child age and interest
3. Invited to a specific Drop
4. Booking started
5. Paid
6. Attended
7. Feedback completed
8. Rebooked
9. Referred another family
10. Captain candidate

### Funnel metrics

- Landing-page conversion rate
- Waitlist-to-invitation rate
- Invitation-to-paid rate
- Paid-to-attended rate
- Attended-to-second-booking rate
- Referral rate

## 5. Decision rules

### Repeat the Drop

Repeat when:

- Occupancy reaches at least 70%
- Parent rating is at least 4.5
- No unresolved safety issue exists
- Contribution margin is positive or strategic learning is unusually high
- Captain is willing and capable of repeating

### Improve before repeating

Improve when:

- Families liked the concept but logistics were unclear
- Gear created avoidable friction
- Timing or location hurt attendance
- Capacity was too high
- Captain needs a clearer minute-by-minute structure

### Stop or redesign

Stop or substantially redesign when:

- Demand remains weak after two credible tests
- Safety risk cannot be controlled economically
- Founder time is excessive
- Parents do not understand the value
- The Captain cannot deliver consistently
- Contribution margin remains structurally negative

## 6. Weekly founder review

Every Monday review:

- New leads
- Bookings and revenue
- Upcoming capacity gaps
- Captain readiness
- Venue and weather risk
- Formspree submissions
- Parent follow-ups
- Referral opportunities

Every Friday confirm:

- Final attendee list
- Emergency and contact details
- Gear reminder sent
- Captain confirmation
- Venue readiness
- Weather decision deadline
- Payment status

## 7. Post-Drop review

Complete within 24 hours:

### Numbers

- Booked
- Attended
- No-shows
- Revenue
- Direct costs
- Margin
- Founder hours

### Experience

- What children enjoyed most
- What parents valued most
- Where attention dropped
- Which instruction was unclear
- Whether age grouping worked
- Whether capacity felt safe

### Operations

- Venue issue
- Gear issue
- Timing issue
- Communication issue
- Captain issue
- Incident or near miss

### Decision

Choose one:

- Repeat unchanged
- Repeat with revisions
- Pause
- Retire

## 9. Metrics that do not prove the business

Do not confuse these with validation:

- Facebook reactions
- People saying the idea is cool
- Free registrations without attendance
- Instagram followers
- Number of activity ideas
- Number of cities expressing vague interest

The business is becoming real when families repeatedly pay and trusted Captains repeatedly deliver.

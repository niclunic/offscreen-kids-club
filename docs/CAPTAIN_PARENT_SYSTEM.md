# Captain Parent Operating System

## 1. Purpose

Captain Parents create trusted local supply for Offscreen Kids Club. They are mums, dads or guardians who can host a defined real-world activity under an approved Offscreen Drop Playbook.

Captain Parents are not informal babysitters. Parents or guardians remain present and responsible for their children during every Drop.

## 2. Captain types

### Community Captain

Hosts low-risk or simple skill-sharing Drops, such as:

- First Goals
- First Trails
- First Sounds
- Build Club
- Photo Walks

### Skill Captain

Has meaningful experience in the activity and can manage progression, such as:

- First Wheels
- Bike confidence
- Advanced football
- Outdoor cooking

### Professional Partner

Used for higher-risk or regulated activities, such as:

- Ski and snowboard
- Climbing
- Water activities
- Mountain biking
- Surf or paddle activities

## 3. Captain application fields

Every candidate provides:

- Full name
- Email and phone
- Relationship to the local parent community
- Skill or activity proposed
- Relevant experience
- Preferred age range
- Beginner, intermediate or advanced level
- Maximum comfortable number of children
- Whether another adult is required
- Required gear
- Whether families bring gear
- Whether the Captain can provide gear
- Preferred venue or area
- Availability
- Languages
- First-aid or professional certifications, where relevant
- Consent to identity and reference checks when required

## 4. Approval process

### Step 1: application review

Check whether the proposal fits Offscreen's positioning and whether the stated capacity is realistic.

### Step 2: short conversation

Discuss:

- Why the Captain wants to host
- How they interact with children and parents
- What they consider a successful session
- What could go wrong
- How they would respond to a distressed child or parent

### Step 3: risk classification

Classify the proposed Drop:

- Low risk
- Medium risk
- High risk

High-risk activities require a professional partner, appropriate insurance and stricter ratios.

### Step 4: pilot approval

Approve one pilot Drop with a conservative capacity. The first session should not use the theoretical maximum.

### Step 5: post-pilot review

Evaluate:

- Safety
- Parent feedback
- Child engagement
- Time management
- Clarity of instructions
- Adherence to Playbook
- Incident reporting
- Willingness to improve

## 5. Capacity rules

The Captain proposes the number they feel comfortable handling. Offscreen makes the final approval.

### Default guidance

- Ages 3 to 5: 5 to 6 children per Captain
- Ages 6 to 8: 6 to 8 children per Captain
- Ages 9 to 12: 8 to 10 children per Captain

### Risk adjustment

- Low risk: up to 10 to 12 with parents present and appropriate venue
- Medium risk: normally 6 to 8
- High risk: normally 4 to 6 per qualified instructor

Capacity must decrease when:

- The venue is constrained
- Gear is unfamiliar
- Children are complete beginners
- The Captain is new
- Weather increases risk
- The activity involves speed, height, water, snow or tools

## 6. Gear declaration

Every Captain chooses one:

- **Required:** families bring listed gear
- **Not required:** no specialist gear
- **Provided:** Captain or Offscreen supplies gear for everyone
- **Mixed:** some items are provided and some are brought by families

The Drop page must specify exact items. Avoid vague language such as “appropriate equipment.”

## 7. Captain responsibilities

Before the Drop:

- Confirm Playbook and capacity
- Confirm venue and weather
- Confirm required gear
- Review participant ages and declared level
- Arrive early
- Carry the approved contact and incident information
- Confirm first-aid kit availability when required

During the Drop:

- Welcome each family
- Explain boundaries and safety rules
- Confirm parents remain present
- Follow the approved structure
- Avoid unapproved progression or group-size increases
- Stop the activity if conditions become unsafe

After the Drop:

- Confirm every family has left safely
- Report any incident or near miss
- Complete a short post-Drop review
- Submit attendance
- Identify Playbook improvements

## 8. OKC credits

The launch incentive should be community-led rather than purely transactional.

### Pilot rule

- Credit value is agreed before each approved Drop
- Credits can be used toward future Offscreen bookings for the Captain's family
- Credits have no cash value
- Credits cannot be transferred without approval
- Credits expire after 12 months unless otherwise stated
- A completed standard Drop should normally earn value comparable to one standard family Drop

### When to introduce cash compensation

Move strong repeat Captains toward formal payment when:

- They host regularly
- Their Drop has reliable demand
- They perform specialist work
- The operational expectation resembles professional service
- Credits are no longer a sufficient or fair incentive

Any cash compensation should be documented and compliant with Czech employment, contractor, tax and insurance rules.

## 9. Quality levels

### Pilot Captain

Has completed onboarding and one supervised or closely reviewed pilot.

### Approved Captain

Has delivered at least two successful Drops with strong feedback.

### Lead Captain

Can run the Playbook independently, mentor other Captains and support chapter operations.

### Chapter Lead

Responsible for calendar quality, Captain coordination, venue readiness and incident escalation.

## 10. Captain scorecard

Rate after each Drop:

- Safety compliance
- Parent communication
- Child engagement
- Time management
- Playbook adherence
- Venue management
- Gear clarity
- Parent rating
- Repeat booking intention
- Incident quality

A Captain should not progress based only on being friendly or popular.

## 11. Non-negotiables

Captain Parents may not:

- Treat the Drop as childcare
- Increase capacity without approval
- Remove safety requirements
- Take children away from their responsible adult
- Use Offscreen participant data for unrelated purposes
- Sell directly to families during the Drop without approval
- Run an Offscreen-branded Drop outside the approved system
- Hide incidents or near misses

## 12. Incident protocol

For any injury, missing child, unsafe adult behaviour, serious conflict or material near miss:

1. Stop the activity if necessary
2. Secure the child and immediate area
3. Involve the responsible parent or guardian
4. Contact emergency services when appropriate
5. Notify the Chapter Lead or founder immediately
6. Record factual details without speculation
7. Do not publish photos or commentary
8. Review whether the Playbook, venue, ratio or Captain approval must change

## 13. Captain value proposition

Captain Parents receive:

- OKC credits
- Priority access for their family
- Recognition and visible Captain status
- Potential merchandise
- A way to contribute a real skill
- A pathway to paid Captain or Chapter roles
- Access to a trusted parent community

The promise is simple:

> Give a skill. Get more offline memories back.

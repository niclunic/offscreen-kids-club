# Offscreen Kids Club Drop Playbooks

## How to use this document

Each Drop is a repeatable product, not an improvised gathering. The Captain may bring personality, but the operating structure, capacity, gear and safety rules remain controlled by Offscreen.

Every execution should record the Playbook version and any approved deviation.

## Universal Drop standard

Every Drop must publish:

- Name
- Date and start time
- Meeting point
- Age range
- Skill level
- Maximum capacity
- Duration
- Parent role
- Captain name and status
- Exact required gear
- Price per family
- Weather and cancellation policy
- Accessibility or venue limitations

### Universal arrival sequence

1. Captain arrives 20 to 30 minutes early
2. Venue and hazards checked
3. Attendance list ready
4. Gear visually checked when relevant
5. Each family welcomed by name
6. Parent-present rule repeated
7. Safety boundary explained
8. Session objective stated in one sentence

### Universal close

1. Finish with a visible small win
2. Gather families in a safe location
3. Thank Captain and parents
4. Explain next relevant Drop
5. Confirm all children leave with their responsible adult
6. Record attendance and incidents
7. Send feedback within 24 hours

# Playbook 01: First Wheels

## Promise

A beginner-friendly morning where children leave more confident on a scooter, skateboard or rollers than when they arrived.

## Recommended format

- Ages: 4 to 8, preferably split 4 to 5 and 6 to 8 when demand allows
- Duration: 75 minutes
- Pilot capacity: 5 to 6 children
- Mature capacity: up to 8 with an experienced Captain and suitable venue
- Risk: medium
- Parent role: present and participating
- Suggested venue: flat, low-traffic area at Letná or Ladronka
- Founding price test: 490 CZK per family

## Required gear

- Certified helmet, mandatory
- Scooter, skateboard or rollers
- Wrist guards and knee/elbow protection strongly recommended
- Water
- No headphones

## Captain requirement

Skill Captain with confidence managing beginners and separating different equipment types safely.

## Session flow

### 0 to 10 minutes: welcome and gear check

- Introductions
- Confirm each child's equipment and experience
- Check helmets
- Explain boundaries and stopping signal

### 10 to 20 minutes: balance and safe falling

- Stable stance
- Stepping on and off equipment
- Controlled stop
- Safe low-speed fall or recovery appropriate to equipment

### 20 to 40 minutes: movement stations

- Push and glide
- Straight-line balance
- Controlled stopping
- Cone route

### 40 to 60 minutes: individual progression

Each child receives one small personal goal, such as:

- Five-metre independent glide
- Controlled turn
- Safe stop at a marker
- Independent start

### 60 to 70 minutes: confidence lap

Short, non-competitive route with parent encouragement.

### 70 to 75 minutes: close

Celebrate each child's specific improvement. No ranking.

## Safety rules

- Helmet mandatory without exception
- No high speed
- No ramps during beginner pilot
- Equipment groups separated when necessary
- Captain may reduce participation if gear is unsafe
- Parents remain within immediate reach for younger children

## Weather rule

Cancel or reschedule for rain, ice, dangerous surface conditions or extreme heat.

## Success metrics

- Child completes one defined progression goal
- Parent rating at least 4.6
- Zero helmet exceptions
- At least 40% indicate interest in another wheels Drop

# Playbook 02: First Goals

## Promise

A pressure-free football morning where girls and boys play, move and score with their parents nearby.

## Recommended format

- Ages: 4 to 8
- Duration: 60 to 75 minutes
- Pilot capacity: 8 children
- Mature capacity: 10 to 12 with enough space and parent support
- Risk: low
- Parent role: present, with optional participation
- Suggested venue: enclosed or clearly bounded park pitch
- Founding price test: 390 to 490 CZK per family

## Required gear

- Comfortable sports clothing
- Trainers or suitable football shoes
- Water
- Shin guards recommended but not mandatory for non-contact pilot
- Balls, cones and bibs supplied by Captain or Offscreen

## Captain requirement

Community or Skill Captain who avoids over-coaching and can make football welcoming to beginners.

## Session flow

### 0 to 10 minutes: welcome game

Simple ball movement with names and no elimination.

### 10 to 20 minutes: ball confidence

- Dribble through open space
- Stop ball under control
- Change direction

### 20 to 35 minutes: parent-child challenges

- Passing gates
- Follow-the-leader dribble
- Target shooting

### 35 to 60 minutes: small-sided games

Use 2v2 or 3v3. Rotate frequently. No permanent teams and no score emphasis.

### Final 10 minutes: everyone scores

Create a final moment where every child has a realistic opportunity to score or assist.

## Safety rules

- No slide tackles
- No aggressive physical contact
- Balls stay within defined area
- Hydration break included
- Age and size mismatches managed by grouping

## Weather rule

Light rain may be acceptable. Cancel for lightning, unsafe pitch, extreme heat or severe weather.

## Success metrics

- Every child meaningfully touches the ball
- No child excluded by ability
- Parents describe the session as welcoming and pressure-free
- Repeat interest above 35%

# Playbook 03: First Trails

## Promise

A small family nature adventure that teaches children to notice, navigate and enjoy the outdoors.

## Recommended format

- Ages: 3 to 8, with route adjusted by age
- Duration: 75 to 90 minutes
- Pilot capacity: 8 children
- Mature capacity: 10 to 12 with parents present
- Risk: low to medium depending on terrain
- Parent role: present and walking with child
- Suggested venue: Divoká Šárka, Prokopské údolí or a controlled local forest route
- Founding price test: 390 to 490 CZK per family

## Required gear

- Closed shoes suitable for terrain
- Weather-appropriate clothing
- Water and snack
- Small backpack optional
- Captain supplies simple map, cards or nature prompts

## Captain requirement

Community Captain familiar with the exact route and basic outdoor risk awareness.

## Session flow

### 0 to 10 minutes: route briefing

- Boundaries
- Buddy rule
- Meeting points
- What to do if separated

### 10 to 30 minutes: observation walk

Children search for colours, textures, sounds or natural objects without removing protected items.

### 30 to 50 minutes: first navigation

Use simple landmarks, arrows or a child-friendly map.

### 50 to 70 minutes: trail challenge

Examples:

- Find three different leaf shapes
- Build a temporary stick arrow
- Identify the direction back to the starting point

### Final 10 to 20 minutes: snack circle and reflection

Each child shares one thing they noticed.

## Safety rules

- Captain walks route before event
- No climbing beyond defined safe points
- Children remain with responsible adult
- Tick, sun and weather reminder sent beforehand
- Route avoids water hazards unless specifically controlled

## Weather rule

Proceed in light rain with appropriate gear. Cancel for storms, high wind, dangerous heat, ice or unsafe trail conditions.

## Success metrics

- No separation incident
- Children complete at least one navigation task
- Parents rate route length as appropriate
- Strong interest in a second seasonal trail

# Playbook 04: First Sounds

## Promise

A playful music session where children create rhythm and sound together without screens or performance pressure.

## Recommended format

- Ages: 3 to 7
- Duration: 50 to 60 minutes
- Pilot capacity: 6 children
- Mature capacity: 8
- Risk: low
- Parent role: present and participating
- Suggested venue: quiet community room, studio or sheltered outdoor area
- Founding price test: 490 CZK per family

## Required gear

- No family gear required unless specified
- Captain provides safe percussion instruments or sound objects
- Volume must remain child-appropriate

## Captain requirement

Community or Skill Captain with music experience and confidence leading young children.

## Session flow

### 0 to 10 minutes: hello rhythm

Each family repeats a simple name rhythm.

### 10 to 20 minutes: explore sounds

Introduce percussion, clapping, body rhythm and quiet/loud contrast.

### 20 to 35 minutes: call and response

Short patterns that every child can copy successfully.

### 35 to 50 minutes: make a group piece

Combine two or three simple layers. Parents support younger children.

### Final 10 minutes: calm close

Slow rhythm, instrument return and one favourite-sound reflection.

## Safety rules

- Instruments checked for sharp edges and choking hazards
- No excessive volume
- Instruments not shared mouth-to-mouth
- Age-appropriate materials only

## Weather rule

Indoor preferred. Outdoor version requires a backup for rain and noise sensitivity.

## Success metrics

- Every child participates without forced solo performance
- Instruments remain controlled and intact
- Parent feedback confirms the session felt creative rather than chaotic

# Playbook 05: Build Club

## Promise

A hands-on making session where children and parents build something simple, useful or playful together.

## Recommended format

- Ages: 5 to 10
- Duration: 75 minutes
- Pilot capacity: 6 children
- Mature capacity: 8
- Risk: low to medium depending on tools
- Parent role: present and directly supporting
- Suggested venue: community room, workshop table area or sheltered park setup
- Founding price test: 490 to 590 CZK per family including materials

## Pilot projects

Choose one only:

- Paper-plane challenge
- Cardboard mini ramp
- Simple kite
- Knot board
- Rubber-band powered cardboard vehicle

## Required gear

- Materials supplied and included in price
- Families may be asked to bring reusable cardboard
- No power tools in initial family Drop

## Captain requirement

Skill or Community Captain who can prepare materials and explain a build in clear stages.

## Session flow

### 0 to 10 minutes: show the finished example

Explain the challenge and safety rules.

### 10 to 25 minutes: first build stage

Captain demonstrates one step at a time.

### 25 to 50 minutes: family building

Captain circulates and solves problems without taking over.

### 50 to 65 minutes: test and improve

Children test the object and make one change.

### Final 10 minutes: mini showcase

Each family shows what they made and takes it home.

## Safety rules

- Pre-cut difficult materials where possible
- Child-safe scissors and tools
- Parents handle any higher-risk cutting
- Small parts restricted by age
- Clear table organisation

## Success metrics

- Every family completes or meaningfully progresses a build
- Materials cost remains within budget
- Setup and cleanup time documented
- Families express interest in another project

# Playbook 06: Little Fire Kitchen

## Promise

A family food experience focused on simple outdoor preparation, shared rituals and confidence around ingredients.

## Recommended format

- Ages: 5 to 10
- Duration: 75 to 90 minutes
- Pilot capacity: 5 to 6 children
- Risk: medium
- Parent role: present and directly supervising
- Suggested venue: legal picnic or cooking area with clear permission
- Founding price test: 590 to 790 CZK per family depending on ingredients and venue

## Pilot menu

Use a simple low-risk menu, such as:

- Build-your-own picnic wrap
- Fruit and herb water
- No-fire snack skewers
- Simple camp-style flatbread only when fire use is legal and professionally controlled

## Required gear

- Ingredients and shared equipment supplied
- Families disclose allergies in advance
- Personal water bottle

## Captain requirement

Skill Captain with food handling awareness. Open flame requires an appropriate professional or venue-approved operator.

## Session flow

### 0 to 15 minutes: hygiene and ingredients

- Hand cleaning
- Allergy confirmation
- Ingredient introduction

### 15 to 35 minutes: preparation skills

Age-appropriate spreading, mixing, tearing, assembling or measuring.

### 35 to 55 minutes: family creation

Each family prepares its own item.

### 55 to 75 minutes: shared table

Eat together and discuss one new ingredient or skill.

### Final 10 minutes: cleanup ritual

Every family helps restore the space.

## Safety rules

- Allergy declaration required
- No unapproved open flame
- Separate utensils when needed
- Parents handle knives or hot equipment
- Food kept within safe temperature and hygiene conditions

## Weather rule

Outdoor execution requires covered backup or rescheduling plan.

## Success metrics

- No allergy or hygiene breach
- Children complete one preparation skill
- Waste and ingredient costs measured
- Families participate in cleanup

# Playbook 07: Photo Walks

## Promise

A real-world observation walk that teaches children to notice composition, colour, shape and story.

## Recommended format

- Ages: 6 to 12
- Duration: 60 to 75 minutes
- Pilot capacity: 8 children
- Risk: low
- Parent role: present
- Suggested venue: Letná, Holešovice, Karlín or a clearly bounded park route
- Founding price test: 390 to 490 CZK per family

## Required gear

Choose one format before publication:

- Family phone or camera permitted only as a tool during defined challenges
- Disposable or instant cameras provided at premium price
- No-device observation version using framing cards and printed prompts

The activity must not become passive phone scrolling.

## Captain requirement

Community or Skill Captain with visual storytelling or photography experience.

## Session flow

### 0 to 10 minutes: observation rules

Explain privacy, safe movement and the challenge list.

### 10 to 25 minutes: colour and shape

Capture or frame:

- One circle
- One strong shadow
- One unexpected colour

### 25 to 45 minutes: story challenge

Create three images that show beginning, middle and end.

### 45 to 60 minutes: parent-child portrait challenge

Only with consent and within group rules.

### Final 10 to 15 minutes: share one image or observation

No public posting without explicit consent.

## Safety rules

- Stop walking before using device
- No photographing strangers closely without consent
- No child names or images published automatically
- Families remain within route boundary

## Success metrics

- Children complete observation prompts
- Screen use remains intentional and limited
- No consent issue
- Parents value the shift from consumption to creation

# Playbook 08: First Tracks

## Promise

A carefully controlled family snow experience that helps children build confidence and positive first memories on skis or snow.

## Recommended format

- Ages: determined by professional partner and child readiness
- Duration: 90 minutes to half day
- Capacity: 4 to 6 children per qualified instructor
- Risk: high
- Parent role: defined by instructor and venue, but parent remains available and responsible outside formal instruction
- Venue: approved ski area
- Price: premium and calculated separately

## Required gear

- Helmet mandatory
- Correctly fitted skis or snowboard
- Boots
- Weather-appropriate clothing
- Gloves and eye protection
- Lift access when required

## Captain requirement

Certified ski or snowboard instructor or approved professional partner. A community Captain may support logistics but may not replace qualified instruction.

## Session flow

The professional instructor controls technical progression. Offscreen controls the family experience around it:

1. Clear meeting point
2. Equipment deadline
3. Parent briefing
4. Grouping by age and ability
5. Professional instruction
6. Warm break
7. Family close and next-step recommendation

## Safety rules

- Professional qualifications verified
- Venue rules followed
- Insurance confirmed
- Helmet mandatory
- Weather and slope conditions reviewed
- No mixed level group that exceeds instructor control
- Emergency contact and location protocol documented

## Weather rule

Professional partner and ski area determine whether conditions are safe. Credits or rescheduling rules must be communicated before payment.

## Success metrics

- Positive first snow experience
- No preventable safety breach
- Correct group level
- Parent confidence in the professional partner
- Clear willingness to repeat or progress

After every execution, update:

- Version number
- Date tested
- Captain
- Venue
- Capacity

- Why it changed
- Result

A Playbook becomes chapter-ready only after at least two successful executions with consistent parent feedback and acceptable economics.

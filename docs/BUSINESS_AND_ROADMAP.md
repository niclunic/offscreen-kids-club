# Offscreen Kids Club: Business Thesis and 12-Month Roadmap

## 1. Core thesis

Offscreen Kids Club should not become only a Prague activity organiser. The long-term company should own the brand, operating standards, Captain network, Drop Playbooks, booking data and chapter system behind trusted offline childhood experiences.

The public-facing product is a Drop. The scalable asset is the operating system that makes Drops repeatable in multiple cities.

**Strategic positioning:**

> The operating system for offline childhood.

## 2. Current phase

Prague is the first operating laboratory. The objective is to prove that families will repeatedly pay for small, parent-present, real-world activities and that trained Captain Parents can deliver those activities consistently without Nico attending every session.

The company is currently a managed service, not an open marketplace. Offscreen controls the Drop design, Captain approval, capacity, age range, gear rules, communication, booking and post-event review.

## 3. Launch commercial model

### Pay per Drop only

There is no monthly subscription during the initial validation phase.

- Joining the founding waitlist is free.
- Families pay only after choosing a specific Drop.
- Every Drop displays exact price, date, location, age, capacity, level, gear and cancellation terms.
- Founding standard Drops should generally begin around **490 CZK per family**.
- Lower-complexity Drops may test below this level.
- Specialist, equipment-heavy or seasonal Drops may be priced materially higher.
- A subscription should be introduced only after repeat behaviour is proven.

### Subscription trigger

Do not launch recurring membership until the Prague chapter has:

- At least 50 paying families
- At least 3 repeatable Drop formats
- At least 6 completed paid Drops
- At least 35% of families attending a second Drop within 90 days
- At least 4 reliable Captains
- A predictable monthly calendar

The subscription should solve a proven retention problem, not be used to validate basic demand.

## 4. Unit economics

For every Drop, calculate:

**Revenue**

- Family bookings multiplied by price per family
- Partner or sponsor contribution, where applicable

**Direct costs**

- Captain compensation or OKC credits
- Venue
- Equipment and consumables
- Insurance allocation
- Payment fees
- Photography or memory-pack costs
- Refunds and weather credits

**Contribution margin**

Revenue minus direct costs.

### Initial targets

- Target occupancy: 80% or higher
- Minimum viable occupancy: 60%, unless strategically subsidised
- Target contribution margin: 35% to 50%
- No-show rate: below 10%
- Refund and credit cost: below 8% of revenue
- Founder operating time: reduce after every repeated execution

A Drop that looks popular but loses money or requires excessive founder time is not yet a scalable product.

## 5. Product architecture

### Drop

A date-specific activity with:

- Clear objective
- Age band
- Skill level
- Maximum capacity
- Parent role
- Required gear
- Captain
- Safety standard
- Duration
- Price
- Cancellation and weather policy

### Captain Parent

A mum, dad or guardian approved to host a defined Drop under an Offscreen Playbook.

### Chapter

A local operating unit responsible for venues, Captains, community and execution, while Offscreen HQ owns brand, playbooks, platform, data, standards and national or international partnerships.

## 6. Moats to build

### Brand moat

Offscreen should represent a family identity: less passive screen time, more real-world first times. The brand must remain premium, cool and practical rather than becoming a generic children's class directory.

### Playbook moat

Every successful Drop becomes a documented product that can be executed in another neighbourhood or city.

### Captain network moat

Trusted, trained and reviewed Captains create local supply that competitors cannot instantly reproduce.

### Data moat

Track which activities work by age, season, location, price, Captain, gear and daypart.

### Chapter moat

A proven launch and operating system reduces the risk of opening each new city.

## 7. Go-to-market sequence

### Stage 1: Dads in Prague

Use the existing community as the first acquisition channel. The club remains open to mums, guardians, sons and daughters.

### Stage 2: Parent communities

After the first Drop is validated, expand through:

- Mums and parents groups
- International schools and kindergartens
- Local family communities
- Captain referrals
- Parent-to-parent invitations

### Stage 3: Partnerships

Build local relationships with:

- Cafes near Drop venues
- Sports and outdoor retailers
- Equipment rental businesses
- Family hotels
- Schools and nurseries
- Residential developments

### Stage 4: B2B2C

Sell Offscreen access as a family wellbeing benefit to employers, schools, residential communities and family-focused organisations.

## 8. 12-month roadmap

### Months 1 to 3: prove paid demand

Objectives:

- Run 6 to 10 paid Drops
- Reach 50 paying families
- Test at least 3 activity categories
- Recruit 10 Captain candidates
- Convert 4 into approved active Captains
- Maintain 80% target occupancy
- Document every Drop and incident

Priority Drops:

1. First Wheels
2. First Goals
3. First Trails
4. One creative Drop, preferably First Sounds or Build Club

Key question:

> Will families pay, attend and return?

### Months 4 to 6: prove repeatability

Objectives:

- Reach 75 to 100 paying families
- Run a predictable monthly calendar
- Have at least 3 Drop Playbooks executed successfully more than once
- Achieve 35% or greater 90-day repeat attendance
- Introduce formal Captain onboarding
- Secure one local commercial partner
- Reduce founder presence to less than 50% of Drops

Key question:

> Can Offscreen deliver consistent quality without Nico leading every session?

### Months 7 to 9: build the Prague operating system

Objectives:

- Appoint a Prague Chapter Lead or Lead Captain
- Operate 6 to 8 Drops per month
- Establish Captain quality ratings and post-Drop reviews
- Finalise safety, cancellation and incident procedures
- Test a family pack or credit bundle before any monthly subscription
- Secure first B2B pilot conversation

Key question:

> Can Prague operate as a system rather than a founder project?

### Months 10 to 12: prove portability

Objectives:

- Select one second-city pilot
- Recruit a local operator
- Launch 3 initial Drops using existing Playbooks
- Compare economics and quality against Prague
- Document chapter onboarding
- Decide whether to expand, license or operate directly

Candidate second cities should be chosen based on unfair advantage, not prestige. Valencia and Buenos Aires are natural candidates because of founder network and cultural fit.

Key question:

> Does the Offscreen system work outside Prague?

## 9. Expansion model

Offscreen HQ should own:

- Brand and design
- Playbooks
- Captain certification
- Booking and payment system
- Data and CRM
- Safety standards
- Marketing templates
- Merchandising
- Major partnerships

The local Chapter should own:

- Local community
- Captain recruitment
- Venue relationships
- Local execution
- Local partnerships
- Incident reporting

Potential mature economics:

- Setup and onboarding fee
- Platform fee
- Revenue share on bookings
- Margin on merchandise
- Sponsorship participation
- B2B contracts

Do not sell chapters before Prague is demonstrably repeatable.

## 10. What not to build yet

- No complex app before manual operations are painful and repeatable
- No large full-time team
- No multi-city expansion before Prague works
- No subscription before repeat demand
- No open marketplace with unapproved hosts
- No large inventory of merchandise
- No high-risk activities without appropriate professional partners and insurance

## 11. Founder scorecard

Every month, answer:

1. How many families paid?
2. How many returned?
3. Which Drop format filled fastest?
4. Which Drop produced the strongest margin?
5. Which Captain received the best feedback?
6. How many Drops ran without Nico?
7. What safety or operational issue repeated?
8. What should be standardised next?
9. What should be stopped?
10. Is Prague becoming more repeatable or merely busier?

## 12. Long-term ambition

The wealth creation opportunity is not the margin on individual children's activities. It is the equity value of a trusted, recurring, international system combining:

- A recognised family lifestyle brand
- A network of local Captains
- Standardised activity products
- High-retention family demand
- Chapter economics
- B2B distribution
- Data and technology

Prague is not the final market. Prague is where the system is proven.

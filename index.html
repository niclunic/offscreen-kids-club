The idea

Not another kids class. A club for real-world childhood.

Offscreen Kids Club brings parents and kids together for small, curated weekend drops: skate, scooter, roller, football, biking, hiking, ski days and other skills kids remember because they learned them with someone they love.

Offline drops

Real-world plans kids actually remember.

Drops are small, curated parent-present sessions. Some are sporty, some are creative, some are outdoors. All are built around the same idea: less passive screen time, more active first times.

How it works

A simple system for better weekends.

Offscreen Kids Club is designed to feel easy for parents and reliable for kids: curated drops, clear capacity, parent participation and memories after every session.

01

Choose a drop

Pick a small session by age, skill, location and vibe. Every drop shows capacity, gear and level.

02

Join as a parent

This is not childcare. Parents stay present, participate and help kids feel safe and confident.

03

Build the memory

Kids leave with a small win, a badge or memory pack, and a reason to come back next weekend.

Captain Parents

Have a skill? Turn it into OKC credits.

Captain Parents host small, approved drops around skills they love. They set the maximum group size they feel comfortable managing and define the required gear upfront: helmets, rollers, instruments, tools, no gear, or gear they can bring for everyone. In return, Captains earn OKC credits to use on other activities, plus status, merch and priority access.
